Why chronological and not random?
    > Most Machine learning methods split data randomly, but that's wrong for time series financial data.
    > If we split randomly, we might end up training on data from December and test on January, meaning the model has effectively seen the future.
        > The is called the 'lookahead bias'.
    > Chronological means:
        > Day 1-----Day 210 (train 70%), then Day 211----Day 300 (test 30%)
        > We build our findings on the train set, then apply them untouched to the test set.
        > If the effect holds out-of-sample, we have strong evidence of a structural relationship.
            > If it doesn't hold, then it suggests that the effect was a feature of that specific time period rather than persistent.

Steps:
1. Split 70/30
2. Calculate conditional probabilities & run Chi Square on Train set.
3. Apply the same median threshold from the train set to the test set.
4. Compare whether the break rates and significance hold up on the test.
