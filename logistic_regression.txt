What does Logistic Regression do?
    > Takes our engineered features as input and outputs a probability between 0 and 1 for your target variable.
        > input: asia_range_percentile, london_bullish, day_of_week, etc...
        > output: Probability that NY continues London's direction

    > So for a given day it might say "there's a 68% chance NY continues London today" based on the combination of features it sees.

Why start here?
    > Interpretable - Each feature gets a coefficient that tells you directly whether it pushes the prediction towards continuation or reversal, and by how much.
    > Baseline - It sets a benchmark accuracy. 
        > If XGBoost later gets 62% accuracy and logistic regression gets 60%, the extra complexity of XGBoost barely added anything.
        > If XGBoost gets 72% there's clearly nonlinear signal worth capturing.

    
What success looks like:
    > Completely random model would get 50% accuracy on our binary target since it's a coin flip. 
        > Anything meaningfully above 50% after proper cross-validation is a genuine finding.
            > Wouldn't expect anything like 80%... 55-60% is respectable & potentially tradeable for financial data.
